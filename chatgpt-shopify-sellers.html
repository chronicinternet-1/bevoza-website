3 Sample Prompts

Here's the caliber of prompts you're getting — ready to copy, paste, and customize.

Product Description — The Problem/Solution Frame
Write a product description using this structure:
1. Open by naming the exact frustration/problem the customer has (1 sentence)
2. Agitate that problem briefly (1-2 sentences — make them feel understood)
3. Introduce the product as the solution (1 sentence)
4. List 3 specific ways it solves the problem (bullet points)
5. End with a confidence-boosting close

Product: [product name]
Problem it solves: [be specific — not "saves time" but "you spend 20 minutes every morning detangling your hair"]
Top 3 solution points: [list them]
Trust element: [e.g., "4.8 stars from 2,300 reviews" or "30-day no-questions return"]
Abandoned Cart Email 3 — Final Recovery
Write the final abandoned cart email. This is the last email in the sequence.

Store name: [name]
Product: [product name]
Discount offer: [e.g., "10% off" or "free shipping"]
Discount code: [CODE]
Expiry: [e.g., "expires in 24 hours"]

Email specs:
- Subject line: urgency + offer, under 50 characters
- Body: 80-100 words, direct and honest
- Acknowledge previous emails — don't pretend this is the first contact
- Lead with the offer clearly
- Create real urgency (expiring code)

Output: subject line, preview text, body, CTA.
Negative Review Response (Valid Complaint)
Write a public response to a 1-star review where the complaint is valid.

Review: "[paste the actual review]"
What happened: [your honest assessment]
Resolution you're offering: [replacement, refund, apology + explanation]

Response must:
- Open with a specific acknowledgment (not "we're so sorry to hear this")
- Take clear ownership without over-apologizing
- State what you'll do to fix it
- Move the resolution to a private channel
- Be 80-100 words
- Sound like a real person made a real decision, not a PR team
